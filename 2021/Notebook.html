Jun 18, 2021
Interview with Emily Chorwood:
“I contacted Ms. Emily Chorwood and asked her what level of glucose in cat urine her clinic finds concerning - a very important part of our project. When blood glucose exceeds 10 mmol/L it is excreted in the urine. Anything above this amount is concerning. Normal blood glucose ranges between 3.3-8.3 mmol/L depending on the source you use. For the ranking system her clinic uses the information below:
+/- : 2.8 mmol/L (trace amounts, not always concerning)
1+ : 5.5 mmol/L
2+ : 14 mmol/L
3+ : 28 mmol/L
4+ : >55 mmol/L
With the test strips that her clinic uses, the urine will change the colour of the strip and that will show what grade/amount of glucose is in the urine. Her clinic doesn’t measure exact values. For those that are hyperglycemic and have glycosuria, her clinic sometimes runs additional blood work such as a fructosamine to get the precise value of glucose in the blood and to help monitor diabetic patients, typically cats over dogs. I have also contacted Dr. Nicole Howard, but I have not received a response yet. Hope this helps!”
